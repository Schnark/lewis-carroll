First, the fish must be caught

Source: Through the Looking Glass (extracted)

“First, the fish must be caught.”
That is easy: a baby, I think, could have caught it.
“Next, the fish must be bought.”
That is easy: a penny, I think, would have bought it.

“Now cook me the fish!”
That is easy, and will not take more than a minute.
“Let it lie in a dish!”
That is easy, because it already is in it.

“Bring it here! Let me sup!”
It is easy to set such a dish on the table.
“Take the dish-cover up!”
Ah, that is so hard that I fear I’m unable!

For it holds it like glue—
Holds the lid to the dish, while it lies in the middle:
Which is easiest to do,
Un-dish-cover the fish, or dishcover the riddle?

Solution:

Get an oyster-knife strong,
Insert it ’twixt cover and dish, in the middle;
Then you shall before long
Un-dish-cover the Oysters—dishcover the riddle!

(From: Fun, October 30, 1878, https://ufdc.ufl.edu/UF00078627/00033/161j, written with some help by Lewis Carroll)
